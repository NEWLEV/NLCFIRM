Email 2 — Day 3: Tips

Subject: 3 financial modeling mistakes that undermine credibility
Hi [First Name], After reviewing hundreds of financial models, here are the three mistakes that consistently undermine credibility: 1. No scenario analysis. If you only show one set of numbers, you look either naive or dishonest. Always include Base, Bull, and Bear cases. 2. Revenue before cost. Most founders build their revenue forecast first and then back-fill costs to make the margins look good. Build costs independently first. 3. Circular references. Your model breaks when cells reference each other. Keep it linear: Assumptions → Revenue → Costs → P&L → Cash Flow. The Scenario Analysis tab in your model is already structured to avoid these issues. Access it here: [Dashboard Link] — The NLC Team
